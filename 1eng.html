Metro station Zhibek Zholy

The theme of this station is as in the name of the Great Silk Road. On the territory of Kazakhstan, the Great Silk Road originated at the borders of China. The merchants of the Celestial Empire carried silk, weapons, medicines, rice, and also exotic goods for that time, for example, elephant tusks, across the Kazakh steppes to the west, to Europe. The steppe rulers offered their services to ensure the safety of trade caravans, in return demanding a certain share of the goods or hard currency. This is how quitrent, or, in a modern way, taxes and customs payments appeared.

The main route of the Great Silk Road through the territory of Kazakhstan ran through the south of the country, from the border of China, trade caravans moved through the cities of Sairam, Yassy, ​​Otrar, Taraz further to Central Asia, Persia, the Caucasus and from there to Europe. All these cities owe their appearance to the merchants, who, overcoming the huge Kazakh steppes, arranged stops that turned into caravanserais, and those, in turn, into settlements that eventually grew into cities.

The main route of the Silk Road in this region ran through Semirechye and South Kazakhstan. The first revival of this site occurs in the second half of the 6th century, when Semirechye and South Kazakhstan became part of the Turkic Kaganate, a huge nomadic empire stretching from Korea to the Black Sea. At the end of the 6th century, the Kaganate falls into two parts - the East Türkic and the West Türkic. Semirechye becomes the center of the Western Türkic, with its capital in the city of Suyaba. A number of new cities are emerging in the Semirechye, and in the south of Kazakhstan, the growth of those cities that have found themselves on the very route of the Silk Road or were connected with it by trade relations is increasing. The largest of them were Suyab, Taraz and Ispidzhab.
